Menstrual Cycle Phases

Menstruation Overview

Menstruation (Days 0-7), commonly known as the menstrual period, marks the beginning of the menstrual cycle. It typically lasts 3 to 7 days but can vary. During this phase, the uterine lining, which thickened in preparation for a possible pregnancy, sheds and exits the body through the vagina. Menstruation is controlled by hormonal changes, specifically a drop in estrogen and progesterone.

Menstruation: Physical Changes

  • Bleeding: Flow can vary, but on average, a woman loses about 30-40 milliliters of blood during her period.
  • Cramps: Many women experience uterine cramps as the uterus contracts to expel its lining.
  • Fatigue: Hormonal changes can contribute to feelings of tiredness.

Appropriate workouts include walking and flexibility training

Follicular Phase Overview

The follicular phase (Days 8-13) begins on the first day of menstruation and lasts until ovulation. It is characterized by the development of ovarian follicles, each containing an immature egg. Hormones, particularly follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH), stimulate the growth of these follicles.

Follicular Phase: Physical Changes

  • Rise in Estrogen: Estrogen levels increase, leading to the thickening of the uterine lining.
  • Improved Mood: Many women report increased energy and a better mood during this phase.
  • Growth of Follicles: Multiple follicles develop in the ovaries, but typically only one becomes dominant.

Appropriate workouts include cardio and strength training

Ovulation Overview

Ovulation (Days 14-17) marks the midpoint of the menstrual cycle and occurs around the 14th day in a typical 28-day cycle. The mature follicle bursts, releasing an egg into the fallopian tube. This is a crucial phase for fertility, as it is the most fertile time in the menstrual cycle.

Ovulation: Physical Changes

  • Increase in Body Temperature: Basal body temperature rises slightly after ovulation.
  • Cervical Mucus Changes: Cervical mucus becomes clear, slippery, and stretchy, resembling egg whites.
  • Libido Peaks: Some women experience an increase in sexual desire.

Appropriate workouts include high-intensity interval training

Luteal Phase Overview

The luteal phase (Days 17-31) begins after ovulation and lasts until the start of the next menstrual period. After the follicle releases the egg, it transforms into a structure called the corpus luteum,4 which produces progesterone to prepare the uterus for a potential pregnancy.

Luteal Phase: Physical Changes

  • Increased Progesterone: Progesterone levels rise, causing the uterine lining to thicken further.
  • Breast Tenderness: Some women may experience breast tenderness or swelling.
  • Mood Changes: Hormonal fluctuations can lead to mood swings or irritability.

Appropriate workouts include strength training and walking
